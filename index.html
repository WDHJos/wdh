Harvesting Happiness Research

In order to get happier, we must know what makes us happy. Policy makers must know in what kinds of societies and organizations people feel happiest. Individuals must know what ways of life tend to add most to the happiness of their kind of people.

Scientific research on happiness is booming, but its results are difficult to overview due to differences in terminology and the growing number of studies, now exceeding 800 per year.

In the World Database of Happiness, we gather research findings on happiness in the sense of life satisfaction. These findings are described in a standard format and terminology and organized by subject, place, time, and methodology.
